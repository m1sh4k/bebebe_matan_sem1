Пусть
$$
\left.
\begin{aligned}
&\lim_{ n \to \infty }\{ a_{n} \}=\lim_{ n \to \infty }\{ c_{n} \}=a \\
&a_{n}\leq b_{n}\leq c_{n}
\end{aligned}
\right\}\implies 
\lim_{ n \to \infty } b_{n}=a
$$

## Докво

Пусть
$\alpha_{n},\gamma_{n}$ — б.м.

$a_{n}=a+\alpha_{n}$
$c_{n}=a+\gamma_{n}$

$\forall\varepsilon>0\exists N':\forall n>N'$
$|\alpha_{n}|<\varepsilon$

$\forall\varepsilon>0\exists N'':\forall n>N''$
$|\gamma_{n}|<\varepsilon$

Тогда
$a+\alpha_{n}\leq b_{n}\leq a+\gamma_{n}$
$\alpha_{n}\leq b_{n}-a\leq \gamma_{n}$

$\forall\varepsilon>0\exists n_{\varepsilon}=\max\{ N',N'' \}:\forall n>n_{\varepsilon}$
$|b_{n}-a|<\varepsilon$

$-\varepsilon<\alpha_{n}\leq b_{n}-a\leq \gamma_{n}<\varepsilon$

$\lim_{ n \to \infty }b_{n}=a$
